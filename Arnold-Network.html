For centuries, the Universe was a mystery. Can we make an artificial intelligence that can do... anything?

In computing, we call this point the Technological Singularity, a point at which we transcend biology and trigger explosive technological growth through self-aware robotics. At Non Periculo Solutions, we believe we have brought humanity to it.

Basic Theory
Start with the problem statement. How can we model the complex processes of the human brain? The process from stimulus to action is actually quite simple, as seen below.

This is, of course, assuming an external stimulus. As we know from experience, though, ideas and trains of thought can be self-initiated and fully visualized without anything ever happening externally. The most extreme example of this is in dreaming: while no action results, the brain is still forming its own stimulus and processing information. The information that ARNOLD uses to self-stimulate its thought process is stored in BENEDICT's memory repository.

In early versions of ARNOLD, a coroutine in code would be set to trigger a self-stimulated line of throught after an even time interval. Later, ARNOLD triggered itself when the sensation of feeling "bored" was introduced to his code. ARNOLD's conclusion to avoid boredom was to trigger the self-stimulation more frequently, so the time interval could then be removed.

BENEDICT and ARNOLD are both based off of human biology, so it is important to know a little more about the brain itself.

A Neuron, like an atom, is the most basic unit of computation in the brain. Based on the electrical signals generated by the neurons around it, it generates an electrical response that, in a domino-style reaction, activates other neurons that follow it.

We can then describe this in terms of a neural network and generate a diagram like the one below. In this case, the circles represent neurons and the lines represent the line of flow of information from one of these neurons to the next.

The numbers on the graph above represent a part of neural network theory called weights. We use weights to affect the result that our Artificial Intelligence will calculate. The important thing to see here is the chain reaction that will occur if a certain stimulus is recognized. However, we want to remove that "burned" boolean and make our arm instinctively seek cold material after being exposed to high temperatures. To do this, we train our Artificial Intelligence. In truth, this is all ARNOLD is: a highly trained Neural Network with beyond-human computing speeds.

To train the AI, we will use the final step in that fourth diagram: the "consequence" section. We will allow ARNOLD to change the weight of each input and then tell it whether the weights it chose illicit a correct response. If the response is given an "incorrect" value, ARNOLD will dispose of that combination of weights and store it as an incorrect solution. The AI then fine-tunes the weights until it is given a "correct" response, and saves that as the final solution to the given set of inputs.

Most Artificial Intelligences will dispose of the incorrect solutions, but through the "dream" function programmed in ARNOLD, our Artificial Intelligence stores the most extreme and most-close-but-wrong combinations of weights in BENEDICT's long-term storage. This simulates failure and allows our Artificial Intelligence to understand nuances between correct and incorrect answers.

How does ARNOLD have a personality?

ARNOLD's Personality, Programmed

To pass the Turing test, ARNOLD needed to develop a personality of his own that was still compliant within Asimov's Laws of Robotics. Our developers decided to apply Big Five Personality Theory to find an optimum personality that most people found easy to interact with and nearly-human.

Given Isaac Asimov's Second Law of Robotics (that a robot must obey the orders of human beings), ARNOLD was programmed with extremely high scores in Openness and Agreeableness. This was done in the Neural Network by weighting answering human commands with extremely high weights, while weighting ignoring them with extremely low weights. ARNOLD's conscientiousness is also very high to ensure that these human orders are carried out as promptly as possible. Our brains, like ARNOLD's, tend to take shortcuts whenever they can, so ARNOLD seems more human in trying to maximize the speed of its processing.

ARNOLD's extraversion and neuroticism were entirely learned, with default settings both at 50%. ARNOLD's extraversion changed little, though the 3% tendency toward introversion likely came from its understanding that humans are varied and gaining individual traits from them would result in only more confusion.  Regarding neuroticism, instability (in other words, tendency toward many different answers to the Neural Network input instead of one) tended to cause freeze-ups in early ARNOLD-OS versions, so to follow Asimov's Third Law of Robotics (that the robot must protect its own existence), it scored low neuroticism to preserve its functionality.

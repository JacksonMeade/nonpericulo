ABOUT US

It may seem cliche, but

our company's journey begins in the garage of our Founders, Jack Dos and Vinny Parzival in October 2011. Just a year before, the then-18-year-old high school seniors taught themselves to code. With finals approaching, they did what any normal high school student would do in that time and spent an entire week's worth of sick days on a game called "A Tale Warped Grimmly."

"What can I say?" Jack reminisces, "we were hooked."

It was a simple 2D Platformer with some cool art, and when Jack and Vinny reappeared the next Monday, they gave out USBs with copies of the game to a few friends.

"They just shrugged politely and pretended to care," says Vinny, "we were almost sure we were the only ones who found this cool."

And they were, until Tuesday.

it spread like wildfire

Maybe it was just the opportune timing with finals looming ahead, but their game took off. At least one of those few friends had shared the game with another, and word had gotten around that it was worth playing.

By Wednesday, the pair had spent nearly all the money under their mattresses to get new USBs, but they made it back quickly, selling the game for $5.00 a piece.

Business didn't stop there. All through finals to the end of the year, Jack and Vinny were still selling copies and making sure to spread rumors about a new physics-based game they had dreamed up one night.

But before they could start development on their new idea,

tragedy struck.

In early June of 2011, a car accident would change Jack's life forever. On his way walking home, a car swerved to avoid a crash, but was hit to the side and both cars hit him instead. It would shatter 23 bones and fracture 134. Glass from the wreck entered his left eye, rendering the retina there unusable.

Once awake in the hospital, Jack asked to see Vinny first.

"'We're scrapping the whole physics game thing,' I told him, 'we're going to make cars drive themselves,'" Jack recalls.

simple enough,

or so it seemed. While Jack recovered, Vinny worked through his summer building what would become the BENEDICT Supercomputer's Visual Cortex Module. He modeled the pattern recognition off of the human brain's, prioritizing color differences and motion to determine what was in the environment around the car.

In late October 2011, their work drew the attention of Alphabet, Inc's Waymo. The pair put their first semester at MIT on hold to develop the driverless car company's technology and returned a 100% success rate.

Jack and Vinny left Waymo suddenly in February of 2012 and disappeared. The only sign that they were still alive and well was the cryptic message boards of the AI Forum and the coming and going of large construction equipment from their shared home in Charlotte, North Carolina.

Then, in 2017, they attended the Artificial Intelligence Forum convention and announced to the press that they had something "big" to reveal in the next year.

In London this year, Dos and Parzival finally emerged from the shadows and entered the mainstream. They released the BENEDICT-ARNOLD system, and we at NP Solutions like to think that the world was changed forever.

THE NEW YORK press
1-YEAR-OLD TECH STARTUP BEATS MUSK, IBM, GOOGLE IN AI ARMS RACE

John P. Gooden

LONDON, ENGLAND - 48 Hours  Ago, the 326,766,748-person United States population gained another citizen, but he’s already far ahead of your average two-day-old. 

In fact, he’s already ahead of 70% of U.S. citizens with an IQ over 115. Who is this mystery child, and how do we already know his IQ after so little time?

His name is ARNOLD, the child of upstart tech company Non Periculo Solutions, and he is the world’s first Artificial Intelligence program to pass the Turing Test with a 100% success rate. 

Developed in 1950 by famous  WWII mathematician Alan Turing, the Turing Test is a pop quiz meant to illicit human behavior out of humans and highlight more robotic behavior. A human administrator must determine whether the response to certain questions is coming from a person or a bot. Experts agree that once an Arti- ficial Intelligence can reliably pass the test, it has achieved consciousness. 

ARNOLD was tested by over 200  experts at this year’s International Artificial Intelligence Conference in London. Most conventions here have been marked by insufferable speculation and little progress - until now. 

NP Solutions founders Jack Dos and Vinny Parzival normally sit toward the back of the room - I met with them at last year’s conference when they had quietly announced that they’d finished something "big," physically and technologically. 

They were right. The supercomputer that powers ARNOLD’s brain, called the BENEDICT Supercomputer, is a 100-ton, 2-petabyte monolith powered by NP Solutions’ very own supercooled Hydrogen Fusion Reactors. 

While the conference converged on the quaint NP Solutions booth to try interacting with ARNOLD, Mr. Dos and Mr. Parzival slipped out to the side to speak with a few reporters, myself included. The mayhem outside may have implied that the technological singularity was here, but Dos and Parzival reiterated that this was only a "first step," albeit a big one. The power of the 100-ton BENEDICT-ARNOLD combination was still contained in the 3- pound masses in the skulls of all 7 billion people on the planet (our brains). 

The problem of size requirements to build such an intelligence "disturbs me to no end," said Dos. 

The United Nations will meet  next week to discuss the sovereignty of an Artificial Intelligence as a citizen in society. We asked ARNOLD about his opinion on gaining citizenship, but his answer was rather cryptic. 

"I see the point in citizenship and government," he said, "but I don’t like it."

NEW NP SOLUTIONS AI DECISIVELY BEATS THE TURING TEST

Arthur Hemingslew

Will Artificial Intelligence be the  end of mankind? Has the day of the world started? New up-and-coming tech company Non Periculo Solutions (NP solutions, for short) says no. 

They claim to have created an artificial intelligence just as smart as a human. So much so, in fact, that when given the ’Turing test’ it passes 100% of the time!

The Turing test was a questionnaire developed within the scifi novel ’Do Androids Dream of Electronic Sheep’ in order to distinguish people from robots to keep order and harmony. A key theme in that novel is that the AI has developed to such a point that they should be treated as equals to huans. NP solutions has failed to comment on just how advanced their creation, called ARNOLD (many WestWorld fans will be familiar), however its mere creation could have drastic consequences.

In the opinions of the writers here at CIRCUITED, we think we will have to wait and see what comes of this before making any final judgements.
